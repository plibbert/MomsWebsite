The namesake of the business is their sweet grandma who taught them both the pleasure of spending time in the kitchen, and making goodies for people to enjoy. She was Muriel May and quite the baker. Muriel lived in another town and would write letters (the lost art) and would always include what dessert she made for Ladies Aid Society, or she would share something scrumptious someone else brought. Of course she always got the recipe for those. When she visited she was most often in the kitchen whipping up all the desserts for holidays. Working in the kitchen brings the feeling that grandma Muriel May is right there.
